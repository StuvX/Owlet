ENERGY STORAGE

Repurposing Orphaned Oil Wells - For a Net‑Zero Emissions Future.

Matching electricity generation to demand can drive up electricity prices, especially when expensive peaking plants determine costs. Adding energy storage to the grid smooths out the imbalance, resulting in cheaper energy prices, and enabling increased Renewables penetration.
Renewables now provide the lowest cost electricity generation across Canada. Coupled with energy storage, electricity can be cheap and emissions free.

Alberta has the best Class III onshore wind power potential in Canada.
Orphaned oil wells litter the countryside, with the potential to be repurposed for AA-CAES.

Energy is stored when the wind is blowing, and released to meet peak demands in the evening.
