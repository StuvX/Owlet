ENERGY STORAGE

Repurposing Orphaned Oil Wells - For Net‑Zero Emissions Power.

Renewables now provide the lowest cost electricity generation across. Canada.

Matching generation to demand dictates electricity prices. Energy storage smooths out the imbalance, providing cheaper energy, and enabling increased renewable penetration. Alberta has 193,976 wells for reclamation, 3,406 of which are orphaned... excellent solar potential and some of the best Class III onshore wind speeds. What if we could make use of the orphaned wells for energy storage? We've mapped each orphaned well onto Windy's windspeed map.


Compressed air energy storage (CAES) is a proven method for energy storage. Excess generation is used to fill the orphaned wells with compressed air. This air is then released through turbines to meet peak demands in the evening. Providing low cost electricity for Canadians and a revenue stream to rehabilitate orphaned wells.

The well is prepared by lining the casing with a reinforced polymer, and then plugging the bottom with an epoxy cement; hence the compressed air cannot leak into the strata or displace any any remaining petrochemicals in the reserve.

During times of excess power generation a compression turbine forces air through a thermal storage medium and into the well.

When demand outstrips supply the air is from the well, reheated as it travels through the thermal storage, and then used to drive a turbine feeding electricity into the grid.

Using orphaned wells for CAES provides distributed energy storage, with good connection into the electricity grid.
